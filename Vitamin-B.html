VITAMIN - B

What is Vitamin B and what does it do ?

The B vitamins are:-B1 (thiamine), B2 (riboflavin), B3 (niacin), B5 (pantothenic acid), B6, B7 (biotin), B12. These vitamins help the process your body uses to get or make energy from the food you eat. They also help form red blood cells.

    Which foods are rich in Vitamin - B ?

    You can get B vitamins from proteins such as fish, poultry, meat, eggs, and dairy products. Leafy green vegetables, beans, and peas also have B vitamins. Many cereals and some breads have added B vitamins.






    Diseases caused by the dificiency of Vitamin B

    • 1. Beri-Beri :- The symptoms of beriberi are weakness and numbness in the feet and legs, swelling, difficulty breathing, and heart failure.

    • 2. Pallagra :- The main symptoms of pellagra are dermatitis, dementia, and diarrhea. This is because niacin deficiency is most noticeable in body parts with high rates of cell turnover, such as your skin or gastrointestinal tract.

    • 3. B12 Deficiency :- The symptoms of B12 deficiency are ; Numbness or tingling in hands, legs, and feet, Difficulty walking, Hallucinations, Fatigue, etc.

    • 4. Folate Deficiency :- Symptoms of folate deficiency include fatigue, irritability, diarrhea, poor growth, and a smooth or tender tongue. In pregnant women, a deficiency of folate can also increase the risk of neural tube defects in the developing fetus.

    Treatment of the diseases :

    To treat a B vitamin deficiency, your doctor will likely recommend that you take supplements or increase your intake of certain foods that contain the target vitamin. And also you must increase the numbers of food containing Vitamin B foods in your diet.
